# Global Localization Strategy

Prontera Commerce is designed as a global Merchant Civilization Platform. Localization must support investor communication, merchant onboarding, platform adoption, marketing, and long-term international expansion.

## Source Language

English is the source language for global materials.

All investor-facing, platform, marketing, and onboarding content should be drafted or approved in English first before localization. Localized versions should preserve strategic meaning, product positioning, legal caution, and brand tone.

## Supported Languages

Initial supported languages:

- Thai
- English
- Simplified Chinese
- Japanese
- Korean
- Vietnamese

## Localization Standards

### Investor Materials

Investor materials must preserve:

- Merchant Civilization Platform positioning
- business model clarity
- market opportunity
- revenue strategy
- current status and roadmap accuracy
- disclaimers around future vision, including Legacy NPC AI

Localization should avoid exaggeration, unsupported claims, or wording that implies registration, production readiness, or guaranteed future delivery.

### Website

Website localization must prioritize:

- clear platform positioning
- concise product explanations
- culturally appropriate examples
- consistent calls to action
- accurate legal and privacy language

Public website translations should be reviewed before launch in each target market.

### Merchant Onboarding

Merchant onboarding localization must support:

- account creation
- shop setup
- product setup
- payments and operational readiness when available
- subscription and plan explanations
- Founder Merchant Program explanation

Onboarding language should be practical, direct, and merchant-friendly.

### Platform UI

Platform UI localization must follow:

- short, scannable labels
- consistent terminology
- locale-aware date, currency, number, and address formatting
- clear error messages
- stable product vocabulary across dashboard, storefront, live commerce, promotions, subscriptions, and world discovery

UI strings should avoid idioms that are difficult to translate.

### Marketing Assets

Marketing localization must preserve:

- original Prontera Commerce brand identity
- Merchant Civilization Platform narrative
- merchant-first tone
- IP safety rules
- cultural sensitivity
- clarity that Prontera is not a marketplace clone, game, metaverse project, or social commerce clone

Marketing assets should be adapted for local context rather than translated word-for-word when local meaning would be stronger.

## Terminology Rules

Core terms should remain consistent across languages:

- Prontera Commerce
- Merchant Civilization Platform
- Merchant OS
- Commerce Gate Network
- Merchant Media Network
- Founder Merchant Program
- Legacy NPC AI
- Official Store

If a localized term is introduced for any core concept, document it with the English source term and approved local translation.

## Roadmap

### Phase 1: Investor Assets Localization

Localize investor-facing documents first:

- investor one pager
- founder letter
- company manifesto
- platform vision
- business model summaries

Goal:

- support fundraising, partnerships, and regional conversations with consistent global positioning.

### Phase 2: Marketing Localization

Localize public-facing marketing materials:

- website landing pages
- product explainers
- brand story
- Merchant Media Network materials
- Founder Merchant Program materials

Goal:

- prepare Prontera Commerce for regional awareness and merchant acquisition.

### Phase 3: Platform Localization

Localize product experience:

- platform UI
- dashboard workflows
- merchant onboarding
- help content
- transactional notifications
- storefront and world discovery surfaces

Goal:

- enable merchants and customers in target markets to operate comfortably in their own language.

## Governance

COM / Ponrat / Founder retains final approval authority over public localization strategy and core brand language.

AI tools may assist with drafting and translation, but localized materials should be reviewed by qualified human speakers before public or investor use.
