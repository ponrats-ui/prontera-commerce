# Merchant Acquisition Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook defines the first repeatable acquisition motion for Prontera Commerce during the Revenue Era.

It supports the Founder Merchant 100 Program and focuses on recruiting merchants who can validate the Merchant Civilization Platform.

## Acquisition Thesis

Prontera should acquire merchants by leading with identity, presence, discovery, and business growth.

Traditional marketplaces ask merchants to compete inside product grids. Prontera asks merchants to build a place inside a connected commerce world.

## Target Channels

Use local business groups, computer shop communities, SME groups, handmade seller communities, gaming shop groups, and creator commerce communities.

Best for:

- Computer Stores
- IT Equipment Sellers
- Gaming Shops
- Handmade Businesses
- SME Retailers

Use chambers, local entrepreneur groups, market communities, coworking spaces, and neighborhood merchant networks.

Best for:

- SME Retailers

Use short founder-led content explaining the vision, merchant problems, onboarding journey, and Founder Merchant benefits.

Best for:

## YouTube

Use product demos, founder updates, merchant interviews, onboarding walkthroughs, and long-form vision content.

Best for:

Invite early merchants to recommend other high-quality merchants.

Best for:

Build relationships with trade groups, SME associations, retail networks, and creator business communities.

Best for:

Use founder-led personal outreach to recruit merchants who match the first 100 profile.

Best for:

- High-quality early case studies

## Recruitment Stages

## Stage 1: Awareness

Goal:

Make merchants understand that Prontera is not another marketplace clone.

Core message:

Prontera Commerce helps merchants build identity, operate their business, and become discoverable inside a Merchant Civilization Platform.

Actions:

- Publish founder posts
- Share vision clips
- Join local merchant discussions
- Introduce the Founder Merchant 100 Program

## Stage 2: Interest

Goal:

Convert curiosity into a product conversation.

Core message:

Founder Merchants receive recognition, early access, priority discovery, and a chance to shape the platform.

Actions:

- Send one-page program summary
- Invite merchants to demo
- Explain Founder benefits
- Collect merchant category and business needs

## Stage 3: Demo

Goal:

Show merchants what they can become inside Prontera.

Demo flow:

- Merchant OS overview
- Shop creation
- World district placement
- Founder badge
- Live commerce direction
- Promotion and subscription direction

## Stage 4: Onboarding

Goal:

Help merchants create and publish a shop quickly.

Actions:

- Create merchant profile
- Add shop branding
- Select district
- Publish storefront
- Confirm Founder status if approved

## Stage 5: Activation

Goal:

Move merchants from registered to useful and visible.

Activation checklist:

- Shop profile completed
- Products added
- Category selected
- Founder badge visible
- World location assigned
- First promotion or announcement prepared

## Stage 6: Success

Goal:

Measure whether the merchant receives enough value to stay active and eventually pay.

Signals:

- Active product catalog
- Repeat dashboard usage
- Shared shop link
- Customer visits or registrations
- Merchant feedback
- Upgrade interest

## Outreach Principles

- Be clear that Prontera is early.
- Do not overpromise traffic.
- Emphasize founder participation and shared learning.
- Ask what the merchant needs before pitching features.
- Keep every claim grounded in the current product state.
