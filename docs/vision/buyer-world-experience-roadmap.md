# Buyer World Experience Roadmap

## North Star

Build a commerce world where cities have economies, merchants have identities,
regions have cultures, and businesses become destinations.

The desired buyer thought sequence is:

```text
Where can I go?
Who will I meet?
What is inside that place?
What might I discover?
```

before:

```text
What should I search for?
```

## Experience Layers

### Enter

The buyer chooses a character and enters without needing an account.

### Inhabit

The character walks through a warm, original, two-dimensional town.

### Meet

Citizens, leaders, and merchant owners give the world a human memory.

### Visit

Stores become interiors with shelves, stories, live rooms, and AI assistance.

### Travel

Warp Gates connect regions with distinct culture and economic identity.

### Belong

Future social and reputation systems let buyers build relationships with
merchants and places.

Sprint 17 establishes this layer through Commerce Square, commerce friends,
merchant following, favorite destinations, guilds, events, customer and merchant
reputation, messenger previews, Founder Hall, and social AI guidance.

Sprint 17A strengthens immersion without expanding the economy or social system:
local player presence persists, NPCs carry daily-life intent, Merchant City gains
lightweight ambience and discovery moments, and merchants receive stronger
personality, quotes, archetypes, and welcome experiences.

Sprint 17B adds the emotional attachment layer: merchant journeys, dreams,
challenges, journals, follow-story actions, local buyer memory, regional lore,
contextual NPC memory, and Founder Hall museum/timeline foundations.

## Regional Experience Principles

- Every region must be visually identifiable before reading its name.
- Regional economics must influence which merchants appear there.
- Leadership characters explain culture without becoming authority over real
  merchant decisions.
- Travel must feel meaningful but remain fast and mobile-friendly.
- Locked regions should create curiosity without misleading buyers.
- Reputation is earned only when a governed progression system exists.

## Visual Direction

Use:

- Warm fantasy commerce
- Handcrafted maps
- Readable two-dimensional landmarks
- Original citizen silhouettes
- Cozy infrastructure
- Magical but trustworthy travel

Avoid:

- Full 3D metaverse presentation
- Corporate analytics styling in the buyer world
- Copied franchise geography, maps, characters, or UI
- Heavy rendering that harms mobile exploration

## Roadmap

- Sprint 13: Enter the world
- Sprint 14: Walk through the city
- Sprint 15: Meet citizens and merchants
- Sprint 16: Travel across regions
- Sprint 17: Build social relationships and civilization foundations
- Sprint 17A: Make the world feel alive through ambience and character presence
- Sprint 17B: Give merchants, places, and buyer memories emotional weight
- Sprint 18: Earn regional belonging
- Sprint 19: Participate in world events
