# Future Trademark Roadmap

This roadmap outlines future intellectual property protection steps for Prontera Commerce. It is a planning document and should be reviewed with qualified legal counsel before filing registrations or making legal claims.

## Brand Registration

- Maintain a clear brand identity for Prontera Commerce.
- Document brand usage, product naming, visual identity, and marketplace positioning.
- Track public launch dates, product milestones, and commercial use evidence.

## Trademark Registration

- Evaluate trademark availability for Prontera Commerce in target jurisdictions.
- Prepare trademark filing strategy for core brand names, product names, and future marketplace services.
- Prioritize registration in launch markets and expansion markets.
- Monitor potential conflicting marks and brand misuse.

## Domain Registration

- Register and maintain core Prontera Commerce domains.
- Consider defensive domain registrations for common variations, regional domains, and future product extensions.
- Keep domain ownership records aligned with Founder and company ownership structure.

## Software Copyright Registration

- Evaluate copyright registration options for major software releases and documentation packages.
- Maintain release records, commit history, architecture documents, and authorship records.
- Register significant versions when commercially appropriate.

## International Expansion Preparation

- Map target countries for commerce, payments, tax, privacy, consumer protection, and trademark requirements.
- Prepare localization, payment, tax, and merchant compliance documentation for each expansion region.
- Review brand protection, software ownership, data governance, and AI governance requirements before market launch.

## Ongoing Protection

- Keep intellectual property documentation current.
- Track proprietary concepts and platform assets in the digital assets register.
- Ensure contributors, vendors, contractors, and AI-assisted workflows do not create unclear ownership boundaries.
- Review legal documents before external distribution, fundraising, commercial partnerships, or public launch.
